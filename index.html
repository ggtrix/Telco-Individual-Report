The median carrier runs lean — limited revenue, few staff, and cybersecurity, networking and billing frequently managed by the same one or two people. 03·HOW THE SCORE WORKS
A vulnerability thermometer.

The BORDERHAWK score is a quantitative measure of external cybersecurity posture, derived only from publicly observable data — open ports, known CVEs, email authentication, DNS records and web findings. It is deliberately the opposite of a 'security rating': a higher number means more exposure, not more safety. A 95 is critically exposed; a 5 is well secured.

Three principles

Objectivity  Scores come entirely from externally observable, verifiable technical data — no self-reported surveys.

Actionability  Every score maps to specific, fixable findings. A high score points directly at what to remediate.

Comparability  All carriers are scored on one scale, enabling like-for-like benchmarking by state, region, size and type.

The pipeline, at a glance
01

Detect raw signals on each subdomain (ports, CVEs, email, DNS, web).

02

Score five independent security pillars (0–100) per subdomain.

03

Blend pillars into a composite using a max-dominant formula.

04

Apply a safety floor and stackable penalty addons.

05

Average across subdomains to the carrier level; map to a severity band.

04·HOW THE SCORE WORKS
Five weighted pillars.

Every carrier is assessed across five pillars, each measuring a distinct dimension of the external attack surface. Pillars are scored independently from 0–100, then combined using the weights below. The weights reflect how directly each dimension leads to a breach.

Pillar Weight What it measures
P1 Threat & Exploit Exposure30%Severity of known CVEs across all subdomains, tiered by exploitability.
P2 Email Security Risk10%DMARC, SPF and DKIM authentication strength against spoofing.
P3 Infrastructure Risk20%Exposed admin services, orphaned DNS, internal-IP leaks, zone walkability.
P4 Web Application Risk15%Active web vulnerabilities — XSS, SQL injection, SSRF.
P5 External Attack Surface Risk25%Breadth of the internet-facing footprint — open ports and services.

P1 carries the highest weight because known, exploitable vulnerabilities are the most direct path to compromise. P5 is second because attack-surface breadth is the strongest predictor of opportunistic attack. P2 is lowest — email security matters, but its blast radius is narrower than infrastructure compromise.

05·HOW THE SCORE WORKS
Not all CVEs are equal.

Every vulnerability is sorted into one of four tiers by exploitability. Each tier sets a severity floor (a minimum score it guarantees) and a multiplier used in the Weighted Vulnerability Count that feeds the P1 pillar.

Tier Floor / Mult. Criteria
CISA KEV60 · 4×On the CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog — confirmed exploited in the wild.
High Exploit Probability40 · 2×EPSS model predicts >50% chance of exploitation within 30 days.
Legacy20 · 1×Older CVEs with public proof-of-concept code but no recent active exploitation.
Other20 · 0.5×Documented CVEs without active-exploitation or high-probability signals.
WVC = (4 × KEV) + (2 × HighProb) + (1 × Legacy) + (0.5 × Other)

A severity floor means even a single KEV finding forces that subdomain's P1 score to at least 60 — the most dangerous vulnerabilities always register, regardless of count.

06·HOW THE SCORE WORKS
Blending into one number.

Pillar scores combine into a composite per subdomain through a five-step chain designed so a single critical weakness can never be hidden by strength elsewhere.

1 Weighted = P1·0.30 + P2·0.10 + P3·0.20 + P4·0.15 + P5·0.25
2 Composite = 0.6 × MaxPillar + 0.4 × Weighted (max-dominant)
3 Safety floor: if any pillar ≥ 75, Composite ≥ max(pillars) − 10
4 + Penalty addons (up to +15, see below)
5 Final = min(100, Composite + Penalties)

The max-dominant blend weights 60% toward the single highest pillar, so one badly-exposed dimension pulls the whole score up. Carrier-level scores are then the average across all subdomains (overall posture) and the maximum (worst-case host).

Penalty addons

Up to +15, stackable, each requiring both conditions in a pair: +5 if any CISA KEV is present; +5 if DMARC and SPF are both weak; +5 if internal-IP exposure and orphaned DNS both occur.

14·NOTES & GLOSSARY
Method, caveats & terms.

This report is generated entirely from passive, publicly observable sources — there is no intrusive scanning, and it does not constitute a penetration test. Scores reflect data available at the time of assessment and may change as posture or sources change.

The estimated breach impact is directional, not actuarial: a communication of relative financial risk, not an insurance-underwriting figure. The real cost of a breach depends on many factors beyond external posture.

Key terms
CISA KEV
CVEs confirmed actively exploited in the wild; mandatory remediation under BOD 22-01.
EPSS
Model estimating the probability a CVE is exploited within 30 days.
WVC
Weighted Vulnerability Count — CVEs aggregated by tier multiplier (4×/2×/1×/0.5×).
Composite
Final 0–100 score from the max-dominant blend plus penalty addons.
Max-dominant
Blend weighting 60% to the highest pillar, 40% to the weighted average.
Safety floor
Guardrail: composite cannot fall more than 10 points below any critical pillar.
